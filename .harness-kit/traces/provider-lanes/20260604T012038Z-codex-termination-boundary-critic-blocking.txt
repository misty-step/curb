Role: fresh-context critic
Objective: refute the termination-boundary gate.
Provider target: native default subagent
Status: completed

Blocking findings:
1. The gate initially treated only pid/Pid/i32 as a bare PID or integer. It
   would miss stop/kill/terminate production APIs accepting u32, usize, or
   other primitive integer process identifiers.
2. The raw OS termination isolation check was too small and could miss direct
   kill APIs such as libc::kill or nix::sys::signal::kill, especially when
   imported or aliased.

Non-blocking findings:
- Live platform files already terminated through TerminationTarget.
- The gate was wired into check-fast, validate, and agent-readiness.

Lead verdict:
Accepted. The gate now treats primitive integer parameters on strong
stop/kill/terminate action APIs as violations, still flags pid-like parameters
on broader termination-named APIs, and scans for direct libc/nix/signal kill
patterns outside curb-core/src/platform/termination.rs. Positive and negative
smoke checks passed, followed by check-fast and validate.
